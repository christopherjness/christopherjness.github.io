Velocity Volume Fraction Stress

Draw a profile of φ on the centre axis, then press START.

This model solves start-up Couette flow with concentration-dependent viscosity and stress-driven particle migration. The top plate imposes shear, momentum diffuses through the gap, and the particle volume fraction evolves over time.

In this simplified model, momentum diffusion is given by

\[\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}=\kappa_0\left(1-\frac{\phi}{\phi_m}\right)^{-2}\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2},\]

and particle migration is given by

\[\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}=\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(M(\phi)\frac{\partial|\tau|}{\partial y}+D\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y}\right),\]

with

\[M(\phi)=k_m\,\phi\left(1-\frac{\phi}{\phi_m}\right),\]

and the stress calculated as

\[\tau=\mu(\phi)\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}.\]

Boundary conditions:

\[u(y=0,t)=0,\quad u(y=H,t)=U_{\text{top}}\]

\[\left(M(\phi)\frac{\partial|\tau|}{\partial y}+D\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y}\right)_{y=0}=\left(M(\phi)\frac{\partial|\tau|}{\partial y}+D\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial y}\right)_{y=H}=0\]
